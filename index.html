How to Play Backgammon

The objective is to move all your checkers into your home board and bear them off before your opponent does.
On your turn, roll two dice. Each die represents a move value. If doubles, you get four moves.
Select a checker on a point that belongs to you. Legal moves are highlighted. You may move a checker by exactly the value of one die.
A move is legal if the target point is empty, occupied by your own checkers, or contains exactly one opponent checker (which is hit).
When a checker is hit, it is removed (for this simplified version).
Turns alternate between players; if a player has no legal moves, they lose.
